Protein – Ligand interaction of the channel forming protein 3a from SARS-CoV-2 using molecular dynamics simulations and docking approach.

Severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) is the viruses that started a pandemic in 2019. SARS-CoV-2 generalize 4 structural proteins, nucleocapsid (N), envelope (E), spike (S), membrane (M) and other 9 accessary ORFs. The focus of this study is on accessary protein ORF3a which forms a functional ion channel, that may be identified to promote COVID-19 pathogenesis and therefore suggested to be targeted to treat the disease. Drug development is very time and money consuming and has a high chance of failure. It is envisioned that, through the use of drug repurposing, the advantage is that many preclinical drug information of clinically used drugs having also accumulate a lot of safety data for the use in human and will consequently increase speed of development and reduce the risks of failure. Ligand binding is proposed to follow the induced-fit model in which protein. In this model ligands are considered to be flexible entities as well as the target protein. In this study, the dynamics of the protein is mimicked by applying molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, while ligand flexibility and protein rigid are used in the docking software. Combining the results from three different docking software for all ligands with proposed best top docking score and decoy docking, BIT225, flumatinib, darapladib and pyronaridine show good affinity and specificity.

About

Molecular modelling encompasses all methods, theoretical and computational, used to model or mimic the behaviour of molecules. The methods are used in the fields of computational chemistry, drug design, computational biology and materials science to study molecular systems ranging from small chemical systems to large biological molecules and material assemblies. The simplest calculations can be performed by hand, but inevitably computers are required to perform molecular modelling of any reasonably sized system. The common feature of molecular modelling methods is the atomistic level description of the molecular systems.
